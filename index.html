So what's this all about?

This is Colorit, a website about color and how colors affect the human body.You can learn how the perception of colors occurs and also find out how animal can see the world, and some interesting facts about color blindness.

How do you perceive colors?

The color vision of a person depends on three different pigments that are in the photoreceptors. Each of them absorbs light of a certain wavelength, due to which the maximum sensitivity of each type of photoreceptors (cone) falls into one of three main colors: red, blue or green. However, this does not mean that one type of cone "sees" only one color: all the photoreceptors have a fairly wide area of ​​sensitivity, it allows you to take a lot of different shades.

How colors affect the human body

Physical effects of color may show symptoms such as an increase or decrease in the frequency of breathing, palpitations, and pulse. For example, red acts excitably on the nervous system, while the blue calms down. The orange color, along with red, stimulates the visual and auditory centers of the brain, which can give the effect of increasing the volume of sounds. And green and blue, being the colors of rest, can weaken the volume of noise. It has long been proven by psychologists that certain colors and shades can not only change the mood, but also the overall health of a person.

How animals perceive colors

The octopus's eye is very similar to the human eye, and although they do not have a color vision, they can distinguish the shape of objects. All mammals, except monkeys, see the world in black and white. Birds are usually well distinguished by yellow, red, green and orange shades. They see the blue color worse than the other colors - hence the relative rarity of a bright blue plumage. Only some birds can distinguish purple color. Conventional flies can see blue color; They do not like this color, because they keep away from the windows or doors painted with this color.

Interesting facts

1. Color blindness is more prevalent among males than females, because the most common form of color vision deficiency is encoded on the X sex chromosome.

2. If a woman is red-green color blind, all her sons will also be color blind.

3. John Dalton was also color blind.
